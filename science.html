The science of drift.

Three interactive demos. Each runs locally in your browser via WebAudio. No samples, no streaming — every sound below is generated live, the same way the iOS app does it.

Demo 01 · Spectral tilt
1.00
PINK

Spectral tilt is a slider, not a label.

Most apps give you a fixed menu of "white / pink / brown". We expose the underlying parameter: α, the negative slope of the power spectrum. White is α≈0; pink is α≈1; brown is α≈2. Everything in between is a fluid blend.

Drag the slider above. At α≈0.4 you'll hear a "warm white" that's hard to find elsewhere. At α≈1.6 you get a "deeper than pink, brighter than brown" — the band most users settle into for sleep.

Demo 02 · Binaural

6.0 Hz
Theta · drift / dream

⚠ Use headphones — binaural beats require stereo separation.

Binaural beats are a perception trick.

Play one tone in the left ear, a slightly different tone in the right. Your brain hears a third tone — the beat between them. Research on entrainment is mixed; we ship it as a *cue layer*, not a treatment. A 6 Hz beat won't put you to sleep, but it pairs nicely with a sleep-mode soundscape.

Drag the beat slider. The band readout shows which neural rhythm range you're cueing — delta for deep sleep, alpha for relaxed focus, gamma for high alertness.

Demo 03 · Generative composer

85%

The composer doesn't sample anything.

Each pattern is a tiny declarative recipe — a list of per-layer modulators (sine, triangle, random-walk, beating) with phase offsets so layers never peak simultaneously. The recipes are tiny (~20 lines each); the perceived variety is large because modulator combinations rarely line up.

Pick a pattern. Set intensity. Hit play. The mix drifts. After two minutes you'll never have heard the same instant twice — and the CPU cost is under 1 % because everything is math, no buffers.
